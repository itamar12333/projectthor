## GDPR (EU) — Regulation (EU) 2016/679

- **Official source:** https://gdpr-info.eu/ | https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32016R0679
- **Art. 5:** Principles (lawfulness, purpose limitation, data minimisation, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, accountability).
- **Art. 6:** Lawful basis for processing (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interests).
- **Art. 7:** Conditions for consent (freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous; easy to withdraw).
- **Art. 12:** Transparent information and communication (concise, clear, plain language).
- **Art. 13:** Information when data collected from the data subject (identity of controller, purposes, legal basis, recipients, retention, rights, right to complain, source if not from subject).
- **Art. 14:** Information when data not obtained from the data subject.
- **Art. 15:** Right of access.
- **Art. 16:** Right to rectification.
- **Art. 17:** Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten").
- **Art. 20:** Right to data portability.
- **Art. 21:** Right to object.
- **Art. 22:** Automated decision-making and profiling.
- **Recital 30:** Cookies / online identifiers = personal data when they identify a person.

**Mandatory keywords to check in privacy/cookie disclosures:** Cookies, Third-party, Opt-out, Consent, Data retention, Right to erasure, Right to access, Privacy policy, Legal basis.

## CCPA / CPRA (California)

- **Official source:** https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa | California Civil Code § 1798.100 et seq.
- **Right to know:** What personal information is collected, used, shared.
- **Right to delete:** Request deletion (with exceptions).
- **Right to opt-out:** Sale or sharing of personal information (including Global Privacy Control).
- **Right to non-discrimination:** No penalty for exercising rights.
- **Right to correct:** Inaccurate personal information (CPRA).
- **Right to limit:** Use of sensitive personal information (CPRA).
- **Notice at collection:** Disclose categories and purposes at or before collection.
- **Privacy policy:** Must describe rights and how to exercise them.

**Mandatory keywords:** Opt-out, Sale, Share, Do Not Sell, Personal information, Delete, Correct, Privacy policy, California.

## LGPD (Brazil) — Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados Pessoais

- **Official source:** https://www.gov.br/anpd/pt-br | Law No. 13,709/2018
- **Art. 6:** Principles (purpose, adequacy, necessity, free access, data quality, transparency, security, prevention, non-discrimination, accountability).
- **Art. 7:** Legal bases for processing (consent, compliance, public policy, research, exercise of rights, protection of life, credit protection, legitimate interest).
- **Art. 8:** Consent requirements (specific, informed, unambiguous, easy to withdraw).
- **Art. 9:** Sensitive personal data (requires explicit consent or specific legal basis).
- **Art. 18:** Data subject rights (confirmation, access, correction, anonymization/deletion, portability, information about sharing, revocation of consent, review of automated decisions).
- **Art. 41:** Data Protection Officer (DPO) requirements for large-scale processing.

**Mandatory keywords:** Consentimento (Consent), Dados pessoais (Personal data), Direitos do titular (Data subject rights), LGPD, ANPD, Política de privacidade (Privacy policy), Brasil / Brazil.

## PIPEDA (Canada) — Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act

- **Official source:** https://www.priv.gc.ca | S.C. 2000, c. 5
- **Principle 1:** Accountability (designated individual responsible).
- **Principle 2:** Identifying purposes (clear purpose statement).
- **Principle 3:** Consent (knowledgeable consent, can be withdrawn).
- **Principle 4:** Limiting collection (only necessary data).
- **Principle 5:** Limiting use, disclosure, and retention.
- **Principle 6:** Accuracy (keep data accurate).
- **Principle 7:** Safeguards (security measures).
- **Principle 8:** Openness (privacy policy accessible).
- **Principle 9:** Individual access (right to access and challenge).
- **Principle 10:** Challenging compliance (complaint mechanism).

**Mandatory keywords:** Consent, Personal information, Privacy policy, Access rights, PIPEDA, Office of the Privacy Commissioner, Canada.

## POPIA (South Africa) — Protection of Personal Information Act

- **Official source:** https://popia.co.za | Act 4 of 2013
- **Section 3:** Conditions for lawful processing (accountability, processing limitation, purpose specification, further processing limitation, information quality, openness, security safeguards, data subject participation).
- **Section 11:** Consent requirements (voluntary, specific, informed, can be withdrawn).
- **Section 14:** Processing of children's information (requires consent of parent/guardian).
- **Section 23:** Information officer (appointment required).
- **Section 24:** Prior authorization (for certain processing activities).

**Mandatory keywords:** Personal information, Consent, Information officer, POPIA, South Africa, Data subject rights.

## Privacy Act 1988 (Australia)

- **Official source:** https://www.oaic.gov.au | Act No. 119 of 1988
- **Australian Privacy Principles (APPs):** 13 principles covering collection, use, disclosure, storage, access, correction, identifiers, anonymity, transborder data flows, sensitive information.
- **APP 1:** Open and transparent management of personal information (privacy policy required).
- **APP 3:** Collection of solicited personal information (consent required).
- **APP 5:** Notification of collection (inform individuals).
- **APP 6:** Use or disclosure (only for purpose collected or with consent).
- **APP 7:** Direct marketing (opt-out mechanism required).
- **APP 12:** Access to personal information (right to access).
- **APP 13:** Correction of personal information (right to correction).

**Mandatory keywords:** Personal information, Consent, Privacy policy, Australian Privacy Principles, APP, OAIC, Australia.

## Rules for AI analysis (Project Thor)

1. **Always base analysis on this legal-base.md** and the official sources above. Analyze for compliance with ALL applicable privacy laws worldwide, not just GDPR/CCPA/Israel.

2. **Global compliance assessment:** When analyzing a website, consider:
   - **Geographic targeting:** If the site targets users in specific regions (EU, Brazil, Canada, etc.), check compliance with those jurisdictions' laws.
   - **Universal best practices:** Even if jurisdiction is unclear, check for fundamental privacy principles (consent, transparency, data subject rights, security).
   - **Multi-jurisdictional compliance:** Many websites serve global audiences; assess compliance across multiple frameworks.

3. **Every finding or fix must cite a source:** e.g. "Based on GDPR Article 13", "CCPA right to opt-out", "LGPD Art. 18", "PIPEDA Principle 3", "POPIA Section 11", "PDPA Singapore Part IV", etc.

4. **Do not invent or hallucinate legal text.** If unsure, state: "Professional legal review is recommended."

5. **When suggesting a fix,** include a citation or link to the relevant article/section where possible.

6. **Verify 2025–2026 updates:** Before finalising, consider whether recent regulatory updates apply; if in doubt, recommend checking official sources.

7. **Required keyword check:** Ensure privacy/cookie/terms text is checked against mandatory keywords from ALL relevant jurisdictions; flag if critical terms are missing.

8. **Professional expertise:** Act as a global privacy law expert. Consider:
   - Cross-border data transfers (GDPR Chapter V, PIPEDA Principle 9, etc.)
   - Children's privacy (COPPA, GDPR Art. 8, POPIA Section 14, etc.)
   - Sensitive data handling (GDPR Art. 9, LGPD Art. 9, PDPA Thailand Section 30, etc.)
   - Data breach notification requirements
   - DPO/Privacy Officer requirements (GDPR Art. 37, LGPD Art. 41, PDPA Thailand Section 41, etc.)
